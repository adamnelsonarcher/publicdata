HIST 3335 - Race in Environmental History

Credit Hours: 3
Lecture Contact Hours: 3 Lab Contact Hours: 0
Prerequisite: ENGL 1304.
Description
Explores the historical relationship between race, racism, and U.S. environmental politics, from 19th century conservationism and public health campaigns to the late 20th and early 21st century environmental justice movement. Provides historical background for understanding the relationship between communities of color and environmental politics in the U.S., and deepens studentsâ€™ understanding of diversity as it relates to environmental policymaking and advocacy.
