PETR 6338 - Applied Mathematical Methods in Petroleum Engineering

Credit Hours: 3.0
Lecture Contact Hours: 3 Lab Contact Hours: 0
Prerequisite: Petroleum Engineering graduate standing or by permission of the department.

Graduate level introduction to mathematical methods that petroleum engineers need to understand for dealing with key engineering topics related to their careers. The mathematical concepts include ordinary and partial differential equations, Fourier methods, Laplace transform, numerical methods, applied statistic and Monte Carlo simulation.
