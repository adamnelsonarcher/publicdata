ECON 4373 - Economics of Financial Crises

Credit Hours: 3
Lecture Contact Hours: 3 Lab Contact Hours: 0
Prerequisite: ECON 3332 and ECON 3320 and credit for or concurrent enrollment in ECON 3334 or consent of instructor.
Description
This course reviews the historical background behind various crises that have occurred around the world and proposes theories to address them. Topics covered include banking crises, sovereign debt defaults, economic depressions, international contagion, exchange rate crises, hyperinflations and behavioral finance.
