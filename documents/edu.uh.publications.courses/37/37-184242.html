PHLS 8310 - Psychology of Learning in STEM

Credit Hours: 3
Lecture Contact Hours: 3 Lab Contact Hours: 0
Prerequisite: Graduate status.

The purpose of this seminar style course is to introduce students to a range of theories and research on cognitive, affective, motivational, and social processes that influence learning in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). Students will read and discuss articles describing theories and research on math and science learning from an educational psychology perspective. Individually, students will focus on a STEM domain and evaluate theory and research on the role of specific psychological processes in learning and achievement within the selected STEM domain.
